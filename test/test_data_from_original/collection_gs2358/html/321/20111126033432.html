    New Reform, New Cultural Legacy

    The Egyptian revolution has been a shrewd experience to the entire country, it consists of healing a corruption which lasted during the last 60 years. It may be defined as an anomaly since Egyptians were living in a censorship of expression, no right of saying no, no right of opining, no right of contradicting, no, no, no… To sum up, there was no right of living if you did not want to be evolved and taken by the corruption that was dominating the country. We were taking in a trap which had led us to go awry. Thus, this rebellion give us the chance and the opportunity to  redeem our credibility and our prestige. In other words, it will certainly affect our cultural legacy, I believe that there are three main ways in which we will recognize changes: calling for one’s rights, patriotism and success.

    Concerning calling for rights, Egyptians will no more be intimidated  to live their citizenship correctly since I believe that the high PDI that was present in Egypt will incredibly decrease. In fact, I believe that mitigation and bureaucracy will not be as important as they were in the past. I believe that Egyptians will be seeking for the relation between work and reward, independence, autonomy and meritocracy. Hence, when all of these will be applied, their patriotism will increase, regardless to the fact that they have been recognizing how meaningful their country is since the threshold of the revolution; and that is what is underscored in an Egyptian song which says “There is something marvelous and beautiful in it, there is something staggering and incredible between us”. Finally, all of these will lead Egyptians to be more aware and to look forward being successful and efficient; therefore, the “fakes” concept will be thrown away.
