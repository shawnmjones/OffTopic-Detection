Created in 1985, the World Organisation Against Torture (OMCT) is today the main coalition of international non-governmental organisations (NGO) fighting against torture, summary executions, enforced disappearances and all other cruel, inhuman or degrading treatment. With 311 affiliated organisations in its SOS-Torture Network and many tens of thousands correspondents in every country, OMCT is the most important network of non-governmental organisations working for the protection and the promotion of human rights in the world. Zimbabwe:  Ongoing risks for human rights defenders in the context of political deadlock and pre-electoral period
The Observatory for the Protection of Human Rights Defenders has published today its fact-finding mission report “Zimbabwe: Ongoing risks for human rights defenders in the context of political deadlock and pre-electoral period”. While Zimbabwe prepares a referendum for a new Constitution and with elections approaching, our organisations fear that human rights defenders will once again fall victim to a rise in violence and a crackdown on civil society, due, in particular, to the collusion between security forces and the Zanu-PF. This report analyses multiple forms of harassment facing human rights defenders as they try to carry out their work.
